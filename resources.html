Tips for Water Conservation

As water scarcity increases around the world, many areas struggle with maintaining a sustainable supply. Water is an essential but finite commidity for the preservation of life. If not managed properly, we can expect increasing water shortages in the near future. Thus, it's important to keep this in mind in our everyday life. Adopting simple yet effective practices can go a long way to help alleviate these potential shortages. This quick guide explores practical strageties and resources to conserve water.


Indoor Water Conservation

  • Make sure to fix a leaky toilet ASAP
    You can lose up to 100 gallons a day with a faulty toilet

  • Only flush toilet when necessary
    Don't use the toilet as a secondary garbage can. No need to flush a ciagarette or tissue when you you can throw it in the trash

  • Shorter showers are reccommended
    Average shower duration should be 5-15 minutes according to dermatologists

  • Don't leave water running while doing something else
    Examples include: brushing teeth, washing dishes, cleaning vegetables, washing car

  • Similar to toilet leaks, check for faulty faucets, pipes and couplings
    A single drip throughout the day can waste 50 gallons or more


Outdoor Water Conservation

  • Water your lawn only when needed
    If you step on grass and it springs back up when you move your foot, it doesn't need water.

  • Consider drought-resistantce trees and plants for landscaping
    Put a layer of mulch around them, which slows evaporation of moisture. Watering plants in the early morning can also minimize evaporation.

  • Turn off hose while washing car
    Only necessary when rinsing

  • Keep hoses and sprinklers away from children
    Also, adjust your sprinklers so that it avoids sidewalks, driveways, and other areas that doesn't need watering
